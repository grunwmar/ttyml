 Linux began in 1991 as a personal project by Finnish student Linus Torvalds: [1mto create a new free operating system kernel[0m. The resulting Linux kernel has been marked by constant growth throughout its history. Since the initial release of its source code in 1991, it has grown from a small number of C files under a license prohibiting commercial distribution to the 4.15 version in 2018 with more than 23.3 million lines of source code, not counting comments, under the [3mGNU General Public License v2[0m.
